Assistant Professor, Department of Computer Science & Engineering

I am an Assistant Professor with a strong interest in Computer Networks, Network Security, Internet Technologies, and modern technology-enabled education. My goal is to make complex engineering concepts accessible through practical demonstrations, real-world case studies, and industry-oriented learning experiences.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe that engineering education should be interactive, application-driven, and student-centered. Every topic should answer three questions:

  • What is the concept?
  • Why is it important?
  • How is it used in the real world?

My classes focus on visual explanations, network simulations, packet analysis, case studies, collaborative activities, and project-based learning to improve engagement and retention.
